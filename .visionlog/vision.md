brutal-forge exists because software quality is a spectrum and most tools only check the easy end. Linters catch syntax. Tests catch regressions. But nobody catches "this UX makes users want to throw their laptop" or "this error message is meaningless" or "what happens when the network drops mid-save?"

brutal-forge attacks from 5 angles simultaneously: code quality, UX suffering, reliability under failure, product feature gaps, and operational readiness. Every finding is confidence-gated (HIGH only), ranked by user pain (BRUTAL > HARSH > BLUNT > SNIDE), and actionable (problem + fix, always).

The output is a ranked demolition report. No compliments. Absence of criticism is the compliment. The forge is honest, not cruel — every criticism comes with a fix.
